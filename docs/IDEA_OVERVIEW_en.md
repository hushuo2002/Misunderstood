# Project Overview

## What It Is

`Misunderstood: Earth to Moon` is a card-based web game about cultural misunderstandings between Earth and the Moon.

Players translate behavior across worlds. A gesture, meal, ritual, or rule may mean something very different when shaped by another environment.

## Why It Works

Earth and the Moon are not just two places. They imply two different sets of living conditions:

- gravity
- light cycles
- spatial density
- the value of water and air
- risk management
- social assumptions

Those conditions shape language, body language, food, etiquette, labor, and governance. The game turns invisible assumptions into playable misunderstandings.

## Current Experience

The MVP is single-player first:

1. Enter the home screen.
2. Select a deck.
3. Read the world-building intro.
4. Choose a team and target score.
5. Take turns against an AI opponent.
6. Read explanations after each answer.
7. Reach the result screen.

## Core Design

The point is not just getting the answer right. The point is understanding why the wrong answer felt plausible.

Each card should answer:

- What happened on the surface?
- Why might the player misunderstand it?
- What environmental condition makes the correct answer true?

## Art Direction

Keep the current space archive terminal direction:

- interplanetary archive bureau
- retrofuturistic sci-fi
- clean terminal-like UI
- cards as archived cultural cases
- animations as retrieved files from opposing libraries

## Near-Term Focus

The project does not need a heavier framework yet. It needs a stronger content and game architecture:

- unified card schema
- unified game state model
- extracted animation timelines
- image generation pipeline
- validation and generation scripts for new cards
